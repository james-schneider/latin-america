My Experience in Guatemala

My first trip to Guatemala was in 2013. I studied at a Spanish immersion school for three weeks. I studies six hours per day. It was intense and I learned a lot. However, I was slightly disappointed in my progress. I mistakenly thought I would be able to speak fluently after three weeks. This is a mistake that many people make when learning a second language. It's easy to take for granted how much is involved in learning a language. In addition to all of the grammar and vocabulary, there are so many nuances that can take years to understand.

I returned to Guatemala in 2014 for six months! At the end of the six months I started to get really comfortable speaking Spanish. But was I really fluent? What does it mean to be fluent?

Fluent - able to speak or write a particular foreign language easily, accurately, and articulately.

Not everyone uses the word fluent in the same way. To some it means being able to hold a conversation. To others it means to truly express yourself and discuss any topic with ease--something many people find difficult in their native language!
